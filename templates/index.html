Plastic Pollution — Quick Facts

Plastic enters oceans from rivers, coasts, and shipping. Microplastics can move through food chains and reach humans.

Actions you can take: Reduce single-use plastics, participate in beach clean-ups, support policies on plastic reduction.

Coral Bleaching — Quick Facts

Bleaching happens when corals expel symbiotic algae under thermal stress. Recovery is possible if stress is reduced.

Actions: Support marine protected areas, reduce carbon footprint, avoid damaging reef tourism practices.

Read the full page
Overfishing — Quick Facts

Overfishing occurs when fish are caught faster than they can reproduce. It threatens food security and biodiversity.

Actions: Choose sustainable seafood guides (e.g., MSC), support responsible fisheries, reduce consumption of at-risk species.

Read the full page
Ocean Temperature — Quick Facts

Rising ocean temperatures change currents, harm species adapted to specific ranges, and increase the frequency of marine heatwaves.

Actions: Advocate for climate action, reduce emissions, and support research and monitoring efforts.
