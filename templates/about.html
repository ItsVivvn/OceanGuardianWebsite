About SDG 14 — Life Below Water

Sustainable Development Goal 14 aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources. Oceans cover more than 70% of the planet and are essential for regulating climate, supporting biodiversity, and sustaining human life.

SDG 14 focuses on reducing marine pollution, protecting ecosystems, regulating overfishing, and improving scientific knowledge and innovation. This website highlights four major issues impacting our oceans today: plastic pollution, coral bleaching, overfishing, and rising ocean temperatures.

Project Purpose

This website was created as an educational tool to raise awareness about threats facing our oceans. By combining simple explanations with data visualizations, maps, and interactive elements, the site helps students and the public better understand how human activities affect marine environments — and what actions we can take to protect them.

The project is designed to be lightweight and accessible, built using only HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and JavaScript. It can run completely offline using local data files, making it easy to deploy and demonstrate.

Practical Actions You Can Take

  • Reduce single-use plastics
  • Support responsible water use
  • Choose sustainable seafood options
  • Advocate for climate action
  • Participate in beach clean-ups
