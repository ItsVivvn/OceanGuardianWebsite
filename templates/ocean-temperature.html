Ocean Temperature & Climate Impacts

The world’s oceans absorb more than 90% of excess heat from greenhouse gas emissions. Small average increases in sea surface temperature can change marine ecosystems, drive species migrations, and increase the frequency of marine heatwaves and coral bleaching events.

Why ocean temperature matters

Ocean temperature influences circulation, nutrient distribution, and the habitats of many marine organisms. Warmer seas can stratify the water column, reducing nutrient upwelling and lowering productivity in some regions. Even modest temperature rises can push sensitive species beyond their thermal tolerance, shifting community composition and harming fisheries.

Observed and expected changes

Global observations show a clear warming trend in sea surface temperatures over recent decades. Climate models indicate continued warming if greenhouse gas emissions remain high, increasing the frequency and intensity of marine heatwaves that drive ecosystem disruption.

Local impacts

  • Increased coral bleaching and reduced reef resilience.
  • Shifts in fish distributions affecting coastal fisheries.
  • Altered weather patterns and stronger storms.
  • Reduced oxygen levels in warmer waters, stressing marine life.

What you can do

While large-scale climate action is essential, individuals and communities can help: reduce energy use, support policies that cut emissions, make low-carbon travel choices, and back conservation measures that increase ecosystem resilience.
